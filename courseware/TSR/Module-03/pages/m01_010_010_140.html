Resources

Here are some resources that may be useful to you in the collect phase

For scanning academic sources:

National Library Board of Singapore:
NLB has a wide range of resources from books to databases that you can use to scan academic resources.

Google Scholar:

Search engine that searches scholarly and academic literature.
http://scholar.google.com

For collecting notes:

Google Docs:
Free online MS-Word like application for writing and sharing documents.
http://docs.google.com

Microsoft Office Live:
An online space where you can share MS-Word and MS-PowerPoint files with your team.
http://www.officelive.com

Wikispaces:
This is a wiki that you can use to collaboratively write documents or plan your research.
http://www.wikispaces.com

For citing sources:

Son of Citation Machine:
This website makes it easy for you to create citations in APA or MLA style.
http://citationmachine.net

Zotero:
This is a Firefox plugin that you can use to collect your sources on the web. You can also use it for citation purposes.
http://www.zotero.org

Citation Styles, Style Guides, and Avoiding Plagiarism-The Library-University of California, Berkeley:
This page has many examples that describes how to cite in APA and MPA style.
http://www.lib.berkeley.edu/instruct/guides/citations.html

NUS LION: How to Cite References:
This page also gives many examples of citation in different formats.
http://www.lib.nus.edu.sg/lion/s/citeapa.html
