Introduction

This section introduces the background of the research, why it was undertaken and the scope of the study.
The problem statement is also presented here. In some reports, the Literature Review is included in this section, too.

Research Methods

This section contains details of how you conducted your primary research, profile of the respondents, number of respondents and the focus of the research.

Findings

Present the data and findings gathered through your primary research (e.g. surveys), here.

Discussion

Use this section to analyse and discuss whether your findings support the problem statement or not.
You are encouraged to always maintain an objective and unbiased perspective by examining both the pros and cons of any issue.
In this way, the conclusion and recommended solutions you propose will be more convincing.

Conclusion

This is the most important section as it provides a summary of the key findings and a possible answer to the problem statement.
Therefore, remember to be concise and clear in your description.

Bibliography

This section lists the sources you have used in your report.

Appendix

This is where you will include project planning and survey documents related to your project.
