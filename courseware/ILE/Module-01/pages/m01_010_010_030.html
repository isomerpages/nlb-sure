Types of Literacy

In the past, literacy implied the ability to read and comprehend what one reads. Today, literacy has many more dimensions. Before defining the concept of IL, let us look at the various types of literacy in today's world using the personas that we introduced earlier.

Click on each persona to learn about the literacy types.

  • Rahul

    Digital and Network Literacy

    The Internet is a one-stop place to find a lot of information on almost every subject. However, there are many irrelevant and inaccurate content published on the Internet.


    Analysis of Persona

    Rahul displays the ability to access different types of web content on the Internet.


    Is he able to evaluate the content effectively?

  • Paul

    Media or Communications Literacy

    Television, movies and radio play an important role in influencing and informing students.


    Analysis of Persona

    Paul displays the ability to learn from the media.


    Does he take this information at face value?

    Does he have the ability to use this information effectively?

  • Zubi

    Technology or Computer Literacy

    Our technological devices have become everyday tools.


    Analysis of Persona

    Zubi has the skill to use computer applications.


    Does she have the ability to use these tools fully and understand their limitations?

  • Irene

    Visual Literacy

    Many people are "visual learners" and seeing is understanding for them.


    Analysis of Persona

    Irene has the ability to learn through visuals.


    Does she have the ability to appreciate the positive aspects, such as aesthetics?

    Can she critically analyse the negative aspects, such as cultural biases and design flaws?
