IL Models

Two examples of IL models are the Big6™ and Empowering 8.

Click on the aspects for more details about the two models.

  • Big6™ Description

    Big6™ Description:

    The model was developed by educators to teach information and technology skills. Students from kindergarten to higher education have found this information problem-solving model useful. Today, many educational institutions around the world have adopted this model as part of their curriculum.

  • Big6™ Stages

    Stages in Big6™ model:

    1. Task definition

    2. Information seeking strategies

    3. Location and access

    4. Use of information

    5. Synthesis

    6. Evaluation

    Graphic source: http://www.d91.net/LRC/KG%20LRC/infoliteracy.htm

  • Learn more about Big6™

    Learn more about Big6™ model:

    Big6™: An Information Problem-Solving Process

    http://www.big6.com/

    This website provides more information about this model, its uses and applications. The Lesson Ideas and Resource sections on the website are useful to educators wanting to teach the 6 stages.

  • E8 Description

    E8 Description:

    Developed by the National Institute of Library and Information Sciences (NILIS) of Sri Lanka, this model highlighted the need for librarians and educators to work together as partners in learning. It is used in South and Southeast Asia.

  • E8 Stages

    Stages in Empowering 8 model:

    1. Identify

    2. Explore

    3. Select

    4. Organise

    5. Create

    6. Present

    7. Assess

    8. Apply

    Graphic Source: http://www.cmb.ac.lk/academic/institutes/nilis/reports/InformationLiteracy.pdf

  • Learn more about E8

    Learn more about Empowering 8 model:

    The IL model was developed in Sri Lanka to underpin changing education paradigms of Sri Lanka. By Pradeepa Wijetunge and U.P. Alahakoon.

    http://www.cmb.ac.lk/academic/institutes/nilis/reports/InformationLiteracy.pdf

    This is a useful paper which describes how the Empowering 8 model was developed. It also highlights the potentials for implementation in schools in Sri Lanka given the educational changes.
