Primary and Secondary Data

Click the data types to know more about them and also get a comparison between them.

  • Primary data

    Primary data is information that you have collected first-hand for your research.


    You may collect primary data through:

    Interviews: either face-to-face or through the phone

    Surveys: sending questionnaires to a target group by post or email


  • Secondary data

    Secondary data is data that is gathered by other people.


    Books

    Newspapers

    Magazines

    Statistical reports

    Research papers

    Articles

    Websites


  • Comparison

    Primary Data

    Time consuming

    Consider if you have enough time and manpower to gather the data


    Secondary Data

    Less time consuming

    Consider if the source of the data is credible

    Take note of copyright issues


    Tips

    Always look for secondary data first.

    If you cannot find secondary data that matches your need, then gather primary data.

    You may also choose to collect both primary and secondary data.
