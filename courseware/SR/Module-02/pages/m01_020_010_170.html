Choosing a Research Method - Quantitative Approach

Quantitative comes from the word “Quantity” or “How many?”

A quantitative approach to research means that we want to collect numerical data - data that can be measured.


It is fact-based research. It is also a systematic approach that involves “counting”, “measurement” and “proof”. The data collected can be easily represented in charts, tables and graphs.


Examples:

  • How many people in Australia are above the age of 60?
  • How many litres of cola are consumed in Singapore in a week?
  • What information do we need?
  • What percentage of the school’s student population own at least one mobile phone?
