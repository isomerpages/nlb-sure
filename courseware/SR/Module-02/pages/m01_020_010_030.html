Step 1: Define the Project Topic and the Problem Statement

Defining your project topic will enable you to craft your problem statement. Do you know the difference between the two?

 
Description:
Examples:
Project Topic

A project topic is what you are writing about.


This is usually assigned by your teacher. If it is not, derive it yourself.

Project topic:

Impact of the mass media.


The project topic tends to be broad and general. To conduct an effective research, you need to decide on what to focus on.
Problem Statement

A problem statement is what you want to:

  • find out (a question)
  • OR
  • prove about a topic (your assumption or hypothesis)

The problem statement is generated from the project topic.

Problem statement:
Teenagers are influenced by what they see on television and tend to follow trends set by their idols.


The problem statement is more specific. It includes some or all of these details - who, what, where, when, why and how.
