Survey Design Issues - The Population

Conslderations concerning the population:

Literacy Rate

The literacy rate in both Japan and Singapore are high. Therefore, the majority of the population in these two countries will have no problem filling up a questionnaire.


Language

In Brenda’s case, the Japanese speak one language, so the questionnaires or interviews may be written or conducted in Japanese.

Brad’s target population is students and working adults so they may conduct their interviews in English.


Cooperation

Brenda’s research is about milk. It is not a sensitive topic. Therefore the target population is likely to cooperate with her.

Brad’s research is about SARs. It is also not a sensitive topic. Therefore their target population should be quite cooperative in the survey.


Geographical Area

In Brenda’s case, Japan is a big country, so face-to-face interviews are not feasible. Telephone interviews and questionnaires may be more practical.

Alternatively, Brenda may redefine her population as ‘Japanese living in the 5 biggest Japanese cities’. This will reduce the geographical area to be covered and allow face-to-face interviews to be conducted.

In Brad’s case, Singapore is a small country and face-to-face interviews may be used.


Summary:

The factors to consider regarding the population are:

  • Is the population distributed over a wide area?
  • Should the questionnaire be designed in more than one language?
  • What is the literacy rate?
    • For countries with low literacy rate, interviews may be more effective than questionnaires (i.e. delivered through mail and email surveys).
  • Will the population cooperate with the researchers?
    • This is an issue if you are researching personal and sensitive issues (like abortions and cheating) as people may avoid your interviews or not respond to your questionnaires.
