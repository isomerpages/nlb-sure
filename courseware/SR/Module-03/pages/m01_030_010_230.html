That is incorrect. The correct answer is 'Pie chart'.
Bar chart: Bar charts are not very useful for showing data as a percentage of the total sample size. They are, however, useful for showing the difference between two sets of data. For example, showing increase of awareness of SARS across a 3-year period.

Line graphs: Line graphs are not useful for showing data as a percentage of the total sample size. They are, however, useful for showing continuous data. For example, if we want to show the number of people that die from SARS annually, from 1990 to 2010, line graphs will be very useful.
