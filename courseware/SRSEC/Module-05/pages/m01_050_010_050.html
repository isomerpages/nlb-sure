Planning Your Report – Know Your Audience

Before writing your report, it is important to think about your target audience. Ask the questions, “Who am I writing my report for? Is it for my teacher? Who else may be reading the report?"

Click on the questions listed below for more information

What does she need to know?

What background information does Miss Wong need to understand the group’s report?


If technical terms are used, will she understand them? If not, the group needs to define them.

What does she already know?

For information that she already knows, Matt does not have to explain or elaborate.


He can make an assumption that she has this knowledge and immediately address the problem statement.

What does she expect?

What does your teacher expect to see in the report?


In this example, Miss Wong’s expectations are, “convincing data and a well-presented discussion”. She will also want to see a reference list with a variety of sources used.
