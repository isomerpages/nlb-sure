Tips for Writing Your Report

Click on the notepads to get tips on writing your report.

Tip 1

Write the Body first and the Abstract last.

This is a reminder of what we explained earlier.

Start with the Body of the report, followed by the End.

In the Beginning of the report, write the Introduction section first.

Write Abstract last as it is a summary of the Introduction, Body and End.

Tip 2

Arrange your main points so that it flows logically.

On the right is an example of how the contents of the report are arranged logically. The background information appears first so that the reader can identify with the problem statement, “Will Singaporean Female Teenagers Buy Jewellery Made from Stones and Recycled Materials?”

Tip 3

Use formal writing style

The style below is formal:

50 female teenagers were interviewed to find out their preferences for costume jewellery. The data collected was analysed and summarised in Table 1… It is recommended that…

The style below is informal and personal:

We interviewed 50 female teenagers.. We are pleased to include a summary of the results in Table 1… We strongly recommend …

Tip 4

When you have completed the report, proof-read it.

Read through the report to check for any mistake. It helps if you read it aloud, and get someone else to proof-read it.
