Writing the Research Methods Section There is no audio on this page.

In this section, we inform the reader of the report what research methods were used.

A detailed account of how the data was collected is also included.

Click on the numbers to see how Brad’s group captured the data they have collated in this section.

1

1. What research methods were used?

Brad’s group conducted face-to-face interviews near shopping complexes over 2 weekends (4 days).

This method allows the group to observe the respondents’ reaction when they view the jewellery samples.

2

2. Who was the target population?

This section provides the reader of the report with information about the target population (e.g. Their nationality, gender, age).

The group focused their target population on Singapore female teenagers between the ages of 13 to 20 years. Being in the same age group themselves, they can better understand the design preferences of this group.

3

3. How many people were surveyed?

This section also tells the reader of the report how many people were surveyed. In this case, the group decided to survey 50 female teenagers.

4

4. The research process

To provide greater clarity, inform the reader of the research process as to where, when and how the research method was conducted. For example:

Where: Brad’s group conducted the survey in different areas to ensure that the population sample will be more representative of the target population.

When: The survey was conducted over 2 weekends.

How: The respondents were shown samples of the jewellery pieces, and asked if they will buy them. They were also asked about the types of jewellery they liked most; and the reasons why they liked them.
