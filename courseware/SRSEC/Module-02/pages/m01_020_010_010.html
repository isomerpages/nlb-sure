Introduction

A Research Report will normally have the following 8 sections. Click on the numbers to refresh your memory about their descriptions.

1

Abstract

An Abstract is a summary of your whole research report.

2

Introduction

Your Introduction contains information about your report (purpose, scope, etc.) and your problem statement. It also provides background information, which will familiarise readers with the subject of your research.

3

Research Methods

The Research Methods section contains a detailed account of how you went about collecting the information or data. In this section you can describe briefly how you collected information from secondary sources such as books, journals and newspaper articles and primary sources such as the survey method.

4

Findings

Present the information that you have gathered from books, Internet and other sources here. If you have conducted a survey, present the findings of the survey in this section.

5

Discussion

It will be good to do some analysis and discuss whether your findings support the problem statement or not. You are encouraged to always maintain an objective and unbiased perspective by examining both the pros and cons of any issue. In this way, the conclusion and recommended solutions you propose will be much more convincing.

6

Conclusion

The conclusion is one of the most important sections in any report. It provides a summary of the key findings and a possible answer to the problem statement. Therefore, remember to be concise and clear in presenting your conclusion.

7

Bibliography

A Bibliography lists the sources you have used in your report.

8

Appendices

This is where you will include project planning and survey documents related to your project.
